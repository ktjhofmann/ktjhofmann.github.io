Potato Knödel

Knödel — that's about as German as it can get. It is some kind of potato dumpling and usually served as a side dish. The recipe below is for about 12 knödel.

Ingredients

2 - 2.5 lbs cooked potatoes (boiled one day in advance) 2 tablespoons chopped onion
1 - 1 1/2 dry, plain bagels (2 days old) 2 tablespoons chopped, fresh parsely
2 tablespoons butter 1/2 tablespoon chopped, fresh majoran
2 eggs 1/4 teaspoon salt
3 tablespoons flour 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg

Method

Peel the cooked potatoes and grate them.

Cut the bagels into small cubes and toast them in a skillet with the butter.

Sweat the finely chopped onion in a skillet.

In a large mixing bowl, add together the grated potatoes, toasted bagel cubes and sweated onions. Add the other ingredients: chopped parsely and majoran, salt, nutmeg and flour. Add the eggs and mix the ingredients well.

With lightly floured hands, form 12 even, round dough balls, so long as they show no cracks.

Boil the knödel in salted and lightly boiling water for 8-10 minutes.
