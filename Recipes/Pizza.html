Pizza Dough

Making restaurant-quality pizza from scratch is not an easy task, especially when you do not have the appropriate tools at home. I discovered a simple recipe for great Neapolitan pizza which requires no fancy equipment such as a pizza stone or baking steel.

Before turning to the pizza dough recipe, another important ingredient is the right cheese. I learned that a low-moisture, whole milk, mozzarella cheese is a great choice and goes well with any other toppings that you like on your pizza.

My pizza dough is an exact copy of the recipe described in this video. If you prefer, you can first watch the tutorial.

My recipe below is for 4 Neapolitan pizza and uses the US measurement system. For measurement in the metric system, go to the pizza dough calculator, select Neapolitan style pizza with a weight of 220g per dough ball and a 65% water content.

Ingredients

4 cups + 2 tablespoons flour (type 00)
1 cup + 7 tablespoons cold water
3 teaspoons sea salt
1g fresh yeast or 0.5g active dry yeast or 0.37g instant yeast

Method

Pour the cold water into a large mixing bowl. Add the salt and stir until dissolved. Add about 10% of the flour and stir until dissolved. Then, add the yeast and the remaining flour.

Start mixing the ingredients in the bowl. Spread some flour out on the table, transfer the dough and knead the dough for about 15 minutes. Form a dough ball.

Wipe the inside of the mixing bowl with a olive oil. Transfer the dough ball back into the bowl and cover it with thin layer of olive oil. Cover the mixing bowl with a dark towel and let rest for two hours.

After two hours, split the dough into 4 pieces and form dough balls. Transfer the dough to a baking tray, cover with foil and let rest for 4-6 hours.

If you bake the pizza the next day, put the dough into the fridge. Take the dough out of the fridge 2 hours before further processing.
